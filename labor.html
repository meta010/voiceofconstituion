Labor rights protection

A large section of the Indian society works in private places, since these are private companies and work, the government's salary and working rules do not apply here, due to which there is a danger of harassment of employees. Many big contractors hire small laborers and pay a lot after getting 10-12 hours of work done, so the constitutional rights for laborers and employees are mentioned. Fair wages and bonuses for employees The whole point of providing service for an employee is fair and reasonable remuneration. Article 39(d) of the Constitution provides for equal pay for equal work. Equal Remuneration ActThe law, under the Payment of Wages Act, mandates timely and fair remuneration of an employee. If an employee is not getting his remuneration as per the employment agreement, he can approach the Labor Commissioner or file a civil suit for back wages. As per the law no employee can be paid less than the statutory minimum wage. payment of bonus Paying bonuses to its employees regardless of loss or profit to an organization Needed, whose salary is Rs. 21000/- as per the procedure envisaged in the Payment of Bonus Act, 1965. For every 240 days worked, an employee is entitled to 12 days of annual leave. An adult worker can take one earned leave every 20 days while it is 15 days for a youth worker. During the notice period, an employee may take leave for emergencies, provided the employment contract does not prohibit it. Prevention of Sexual Harassment at Workplace Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention)(Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 protects women from sexual harassment at workplace. The Indian Penal Code also provides for imprisonment of up to three years, with or without fine, for sexual assault.

Right of Elders

At present, the elderly are facing many problems, such as lack of physical, social, emotional and financial support. To solve this problem, the Government of India has enacted the Maintenance and Welfare of Voters and Senior Citizens Act 2007 under Article 41 of the Constitution. purpose of this lawTo ensure more effective provisions for the maintenance and welfare of parents and senior citizens recognized under the Constitution. ArticleUnder Article 41, the Directive Principles of State Policy impose a duty on the State to adopt such effective measures within the limits of its economic capacity and development, in which meaningful solutions can be found in cases like old age, disease and disability. ipc sectionThere is a provision under 125 (1) that if parents are not able to take care of themselves, then their sons and daughters will take care of them. , Stream2(h) defines a senior citizen as any person in India who is 60 years of age or more. Under this, the state government will make arrangements for various services provided in old age homes, which are necessary for medical care and means of entertainment for the residents of such homes. certain other rights The claim for maintenance shall be presented before the Special Tribunal constituted under the Act, which is headed by the Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO). The proceedings for claiming maintenance shall be initiated against any child or relative in the district where the parent or senior citizen currently resides, or have resided before or where their children or relatives are residing. The effect of the maintenance order passed by the tribunal will be similar to section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 i.e. one month's imprisonment and recovery warrants can also be issued. As per the Act 2007, the claim for maintenance can be filed by the senior citizen himself. If the senior citizen is unable to file, he can authorize a person or organization (registered under the Societies Act, 1860) to file the claim or the Maintenance Tribunal itself can take cognizance. Senior citizens can claim maintenance under either of the above two Acts. The state government can fix the maximum limit of maintenance monthly allowance. If the senior citizen is aggrieved by the order of the Tribunal, he can file an appeal before the Appellate Tribunal within 60 days. As per section 16(5) of the Act, the Appellate Tribunal shall endeavor to dispose of the appeal within one month from the receipt of the appeal. If any kind of legal aid is required, free legal aid will be made available from the District Legal Services Authority. In the cases pending in the courts, the cases of the elderly are heard on the basis of priority.
